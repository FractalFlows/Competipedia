about

Every year there are more than 2 million articles that are published in scientific peer-review journals. Unfortunately, only a small fraction of those will be reproducible. Even worst, hidden under those millions of articles there is an even greater number of hypothesis, methodologies, simulation results, experimental results, analysis, datasets, mathematical formulations, assumptions, scripts, source code, etc. (a.k.a knowledge bits) that will never see the light of day, and will slowly rote, submerged, deep inside the abyss of the authors' brains or hard disks. These knowledge bits could be extremely important to assess the reproducibility of the articles, and to actually make the articles more useful. But unfortunately, for multiple-reasons, the system discourage the authors to share those Knowledge Products. Those two effects combined, create a tsunamy of low quality publications, which saturates the attention of engineers, reduces the speed of scientific progress and waste an enormous amount of time and money for society.

We, at FractalFlows, are developing a web app called Emergence that allows anyone to review articles and most importantly reproduce results, data and source code featured in an article. Thus (1) making it easier for engineers, scientists, researchers and students to search, discover, collaborate, share, acquire and exchange the necessary knowledge bits revolving around a given article, and (2) making the article significantly more useful!

Emergence is integrated with Github and the network DOI (..and more to come). Integrations with external tools let you automatically pull information from outside tools into Emergence in a way that's timely, relevant, and searchable.
